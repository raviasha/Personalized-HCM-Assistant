# Acme Corp 401(k) Retirement Savings Plan

## Overview

Acme Corp's 401(k) plan helps employees build long-term financial security by offering tax-advantaged retirement savings with a generous company match. The plan is administered through Fidelity Investments. All regular full-time and part-time employees (20+ hours/week) are eligible to participate beginning on their **first day of employment**.

---

## Contribution Limits (2026)

The IRS sets annual contribution limits for 401(k) plans. For 2026:

- **Employee contribution limit:** $23,500
- **Catch-up contribution limit (age 50 or older):** $7,500 additional, for a total of **$31,000**
- **Super catch-up contribution (ages 60–63):** $11,250 additional (SECURE 2.0 Act), for a total of **$34,750**
- **Combined employer + employee limit (Section 415 limit):** $70,000

You may contribute a percentage of your pre-tax salary, after-tax (Roth), or a combination of both, up to the IRS limit.

---

## Company Match

Acme Corp provides a **dollar-for-dollar match on the first 4% of your eligible compensation** that you contribute to the 401(k) plan each pay period.

**Example:** If your annual salary is $80,000 and you contribute 4% ($3,200), Acme Corp contributes an additional $3,200 — free money toward your retirement.

- If you contribute **less than 4%**, you only receive a partial match.
- If you contribute **more than 4%**, the match is still capped at 4% of your compensation.
- Match contributions are made each pay period, not as a lump sum at year-end.

> **Tip for new employees:** To maximize the full company match, ensure your contribution rate is set to at least 4% as soon as possible. Not contributing at least 4% means leaving free compensation on the table.

---

## Vesting Schedule

**Your own contributions** are always 100% vested immediately — that money is always yours.

**Company match contributions** are subject to a graded vesting schedule:

| Years of Service | Vested Percentage |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Less than 1 year | 0% |
| 1 year | 20% |
| 2 years | 40% |
| 3 years | 60% |
| 4 years | 80% |
| 5 years or more | 100% |

If you leave Acme Corp before your match is fully vested, you forfeit the unvested portion.

## Traditional (Pre-Tax) vs. Roth (After-Tax) Contributions

### Traditional Pre-Tax 401(k)
- Contributions reduce your taxable income today
- Investments grow tax-deferred
- Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income
- Best for employees who expect to be in a **lower tax bracket in retirement**

### Roth 401(k)
- Contributions are made with after-tax dollars — no tax break today
- Investments grow **tax-free**
- Qualified withdrawals in retirement are **completely tax-free**
- Best for employees who expect to be in a **higher tax bracket in retirement** (common for younger, lower-income employees early in their careers)
- No income limits apply to Roth 401(k) contributions (unlike Roth IRA)

You may split contributions between Traditional and Roth in any proportion, as long as the total doesn't exceed IRS limits.

## Investment Options

Fidelity offers a range of investment options including:
- **Target-date funds** (e.g., Fidelity Freedom 2055 Fund) — automatically rebalance as you approach retirement
- **Index funds** — low-cost funds tracking the S&P 500, total market, international markets, and bond markets
- **Actively managed funds** — growth, value, and income funds
- **Stable value fund** — for capital preservation near retirement

Acme Corp's default investment for new participants is the **age-appropriate Target-Date Fund** if no election is made.

---

## Auto-Enrollment and Auto-Escalation

New employees are **automatically enrolled at a 3% pre-tax contribution rate** starting 30 days after their hire date, unless they opt out or change their election.

The **auto-escalation** feature increases your contribution rate by **1% per year** (in January) up to a maximum of 10%, until you manually override it. This makes it easy to gradually increase savings without a lifestyle shock.

## Withdrawals and Loans

### Early Withdrawal
Withdrawals before age 59½ are generally subject to:
- Ordinary income taxes on the withdrawn amount (for traditional contributions)
- A **10% early withdrawal penalty** (exceptions apply: disability, death, SEPP)

### Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)
Per IRS rules, you must begin taking RMDs starting at **age 73** (SECURE 2.0 Act). Roth 401(k) accounts are also subject to RMDs while you're employed by Acme Corp (unlike Roth IRAs).

### 401(k) Loans
You may borrow up to **50% of your vested account balance, up to $50,000**, repayable over 5 years through payroll deductions. Loans are generally not recommended as they reduce your invested balance and must be repaid in full if you leave the company.
