# Acme Corp FSA & HSA Accounts

## Overview

Acme Corp offers tax-advantaged spending accounts to help employees pay for eligible healthcare and dependent care expenses with pre-tax dollars, reducing their taxable income. The available account types depend on your medical plan election:

| Account Type | Available With |
|-------------|---------------|
| Health Savings Account (HSA) | Basic Individual Plan (HDHP) only |
| Healthcare Flexible Spending Account (FSA) | Standard Plan or Premium Family Plan |
| Dependent Care FSA (DCFSA) | Any plan (or no plan) |
| Limited-Purpose FSA (LPFSA) | HSA holders only — covers dental & vision only |

---

## Health Savings Account (HSA)

### What Is an HSA?

An HSA is a personal savings account that lets you set aside pre-tax money to pay for qualified medical expenses. Unlike an FSA, the money rolls over year after year and can be invested for retirement. Many financial advisors refer to it as a "triple tax advantage":
1. **Contributions are tax-free** (pre-tax payroll deductions or tax-deductible if made directly)
2. **Earnings grow tax-free** (interest and investment gains are not taxed)
3. **Withdrawals are tax-free** when used for qualified medical expenses

### Eligibility Requirements

To open and contribute to an HSA, you must:
- Be enrolled in a **qualifying High-Deductible Health Plan (HDHP)** — at Acme Corp, this is the **Basic Individual Plan**
- **Not** be enrolled in Medicare Part A or B
- **Not** be claimed as a dependent on someone else's tax return
- **Not** be covered by another non-HDHP health plan (including your spouse's FSA, except a Limited-Purpose FSA)

### 2026 Contribution Limits (IRS)

- **Self-only coverage:** $4,400
- **Family coverage:** $8,750
- **Catch-up contribution (age 55+):** $1,000 additional

These limits include both your contributions and any Acme Corp contribution.

### Acme Corp HSA Seed Contribution

Acme Corp contributes a **$500 seed contribution** (self-only) or **$1,000 seed contribution** (family) to your HSA annually, deposited in January. This counts toward your IRS annual limit.

### Qualified Medical Expenses

HSA funds can be used tax-free for a broad range of IRS-defined qualified medical expenses, including:
- Doctor visits, specialist visits, urgent care, and ER copays/deductibles
- Prescription drugs and over-the-counter medications (OTC meds are eligible since 2020)
- Dental care: cleanings, fillings, crowns, orthodontia, dentures
- Vision care: eye exams, glasses, contacts, LASIK
- Mental health therapy visits
- Medical equipment: CPAP machines, hearing aids, wheelchair
- Feminine hygiene products (since 2020)
- COVID-19 testing and PPE
- Lab fees and diagnostic imaging

**Not eligible:** cosmetic surgery, gym memberships (unless prescribed), COBRA premiums while employed, health club dues.

### HSA as a Retirement Account

After age 65, HSA funds can be withdrawn for **any purpose** (not just medical) and are taxed at your ordinary income rate — similar to a traditional IRA. This makes the HSA also an excellent supplemental retirement savings vehicle.

Tax-free medical withdrawals remain available at any age with no dollar limit.

### Investment Options

Once your HSA balance exceeds **$1,000**, you can invest the amount above $1,000 in a curated menu of Fidelity mutual funds. Invested funds continue to grow tax-free.

### HSA Portability

Your HSA is **yours — it travels with you**. If you change jobs, change plans, or retire, the HSA stays with you. You can no longer contribute if you no longer have an HDHP, but you can always spend the existing balance on qualified expenses.

## Healthcare Flexible Spending Account (FSA)

### What Is a Healthcare FSA?

A Healthcare FSA lets you set aside pre-tax dollars from each paycheck to pay for eligible medical expenses. Unlike an HSA, you access the **full annual election amount on Day 1** of the plan year (the FSA is front-funded).

### 2026 Contribution Limit (IRS)

- **Annual maximum:** $3,300 per employee (not per household)

### Use-It-or-Lose-It Rule and Rollover

The FSA has a **"use-it-or-lose-it"** rule: funds not used by the deadline are forfeited. Acme Corp offers a **$640 rollover** (maximum allowed by IRS for 2026): any unused balance up to $640 as of December 31 is automatically rolled over to the following plan year. Amounts above $640 are forfeited.

**Tip:** Plan your FSA election carefully based on projected medical expenses. It's better to elect slightly less than potentially forfeit.

### Eligible Expenses

Same as HSA eligible expenses (see above), except that FSA funds cannot be used for insurance premiums.

### How to Use Your FSA

- **FSA debit card:** Use directly at eligible providers and pharmacies
- **Manual reimbursement:** Submit receipts through the WageWorks portal at mywageworks.com
- **Deadline for claims:** March 31 of the following plan year (90-day run-out period after December 31)

## Dependent Care FSA (DCFSA)

A Dependent Care FSA is separate from the Healthcare FSA and covers eligible child or elder care expenses, allowing you to pay for care tax-free.

### 2026 Contribution Limits

- **Single or married filing jointly:** Up to $5,000 per household
- **Married filing separately:** Up to $2,500

### Eligible Dependent Care Expenses

- Daycare and preschool (for children under age 13)
- Before and after-school programs
- Summer day camps (not overnight camps)
- Elder care (for a dependent adult who lives with you and cannot care for themselves)
- In-home babysitter or au pair costs

**Not eligible:** overnight camps, tutoring, school tuition (K-12).

### Enrollment

DCFSA is available to any employee, regardless of health plan enrollment, during new hire enrollment or Open Enrollment.

## Limited-Purpose FSA (LPFSA)

Employees with an HSA may open a **Limited-Purpose FSA** to use pre-tax dollars for dental and vision expenses only. This preserves HSA funds for other medical expenses or investment growth.

- **Annual maximum:** $3,300 (same as Healthcare FSA)
- **Eligible expenses:** Dental and vision only (cleanings, glasses, contacts, orthodontia, etc.)
- **Use-it-or-lose-it:** Same $640 rollover rule applies
