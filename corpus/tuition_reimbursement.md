# Acme Corp Tuition Reimbursement & Professional Development

## Overview

Acme Corp invests in the growth and career advancement of its employees through tuition reimbursement and a range of professional development benefits. We believe continuous learning drives both personal fulfillment and company innovation.

All programs described here are available to regular full-time employees. Part-time employees (20+ hours/week) are eligible for professional development stipends at 50% of the stated amounts, and are not eligible for tuition reimbursement during their first year.

---

## Tuition Reimbursement Program

### Eligibility

To be eligible for tuition reimbursement:
- You must have completed **at least 12 months of continuous full-time service** at Acme Corp
- You must be currently employed in good standing (not on a Performance Improvement Plan)
- The course or program must be **pre-approved by your manager and HR** before you enroll (retroactive requests are not approved)
- The program must be at an **accredited college, university, or trade school**

### Covered Programs

Acme Corp reimburses tuition for programs that are relevant to your current role or a future role at Acme Corp:
- **Undergraduate coursework:** Applicable to an accredited degree or certificate program
- **Graduate degrees:** MBA, MS, MA, and other graduate programs relevant to your discipline
- **Professional certificates:** Job-relevant certificate programs from accredited institutions
- **Online degree programs:** Fully accredited online programs (e.g., Coursera degrees, edX MicroMasters when offered through a partner university)

> **Note:** Purely recreational or hobby-oriented coursework is not eligible, even from accredited institutions.

### Reimbursement Limits

| Program Type | Annual Maximum |
|-------------|----------------|
| Undergraduate coursework | $5,250 per calendar year |
| Graduate programs | $7,500 per calendar year |
| Professional certificates | $3,000 per calendar year |

The $5,250 undergraduate limit is set at the IRS tax-exclusion threshold under IRC Section 127 — amounts at or below this limit are **not included in your taxable income**. Graduate reimbursements above $5,250 are subject to income tax.

### Eligible Tuition Expenses

- **Tuition and required fees** (enrollment fee, lab fees, exam fees)
- **Required textbooks and course materials** (receipts required; optional materials are not covered)
- **One required exam/certification fee** per approved program per year

**Not covered:** room and board, transportation, technology purchases, elective course materials, application fees.

### Minimum Grade Requirement

To receive reimbursement, you must achieve:
- **B (3.0) or higher** for graduate courses
- **C (2.0) or higher** for undergraduate courses
- **Pass** for pass/fail courses

Proof of grade (an official transcript or grade report) must be submitted within **60 days of course completion**.

### Repayment Agreement

If you voluntarily leave Acme Corp within **24 months** of receiving tuition reimbursement, you must repay a prorated amount:
- Departed within 12 months of reimbursement: Repay **100%**
- Departed 12–24 months after reimbursement: Repay **50%**
- Departed 24+ months after reimbursement: No repayment required

This is deducted from your final paycheck or invoiced separately. The repayment obligation is noted in the Tuition Reimbursement Agreement you sign before approval.

### How to Apply

1. Identify a program and course(s) relevant to your career path
2. Discuss with your manager and get verbal approval
3. Submit a **Tuition Reimbursement Request** in Workday (under Learning & Development) with:
   - Course/program name and school
   - Estimated cost (tuition + required fees)
   - Description of how it supports your career at Acme Corp
4. Await formal approval from your manager and HR (allow 10 business days)
5. Enroll and pay out-of-pocket
6. Upon completion, submit an official transcript and receipts in Workday within 60 days

Approved reimbursements are paid via payroll in the next available cycle after submission.

## Professional Development Stipend

All full-time employees receive a **$1,500 annual professional development stipend** (pro-rated for new hires based on hire date) for work-relevant learning and skill-building activities that don't fall under the tuition reimbursement program.

### Eligible Uses

- **Online learning platforms:** Coursera, LinkedIn Learning, Udemy, Pluralsight, O'Reilly, Codecademy (professional subscription)
- **Industry conferences:** Registration fees (up to $800 per event); travel expenses are handled separately through the Travel & Expense policy
- **Professional certifications:** Exam prep materials, vouchers, and exam fees (e.g., PMP, CPA, CFA, AWS Certified, Six Sigma, SHRM)
- **Professional association memberships:** Annual dues for relevant industry organizations (e.g., ACM, IEEE, SHRM, AICPA, PMI)
- **Books:** Work-relevant books and publications (receipts required; max $200/year for book purchases)
- **Workshops and seminars:** In-person or virtual (not covered under conference budget)

### How to Use

- Submit expenses through Workday under **"Professional Development Stipend"** within 60 days of purchase
- Attach receipts; manager approval is required for amounts over $500 single purchase
- Stipend does not carry over to the next calendar year — use it or lose it by December 31
- Reimbursed through payroll, not taxable income (within limits of IRC Section 132)

## Internal Learning Resources (No-Cost)

Acme Corp provides the following learning resources at no cost to all employees:

- **LinkedIn Learning:** Full access to the LinkedIn Learning library (10,000+ courses) via SSO at learning.acmecorp.com
- **Internal workshops:** Monthly lunch-and-learn sessions on topics ranging from Excel skills, leadership, DEI, and technical skills (calendar on the intranet)
- **Mentorship program:** 6-month structured mentorship pairings — applications open in January and July each year
- **Manager development track:** 12-week cohort program for new and aspiring managers; enrollments once per year in Q2
